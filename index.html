Safer Planning Using a Hierarchical Reinforcement-Learning Agent
Project write-up for winter 2022 ROB 520 course @ UofM
A class project in which I explored the potential of finding a safe path while using reinforcement learning to learn dynamics.
I developed a Rapidly-Exploring Random-Tree (RRT) algorithm that used the intermediate layers of a Hierarchical-Actor-Critic (HAC) agent to model state propagation. The RRT would discard proposed next steps if they led to high Q-values. Attempting to reach random goals exposes less risky paths.

An Exploration of Probability-Hypothesis-Density Filters.
code
To augment a course that does not explore the assumption of having no association between sensor information and tracked object, my group explored PHD filters. The filter models the extent of association as a Gaussian distribution. The project showed the success of the filter in certain settings and the general difficulty of estimating people (highly deformable objects) using low dimensional (bounding box) tracks.
